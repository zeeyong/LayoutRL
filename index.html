Abstract

Large-scale 3D layout optimization requires simultaneous satisfaction of multiple heterogeneous constraints while optimizing a multi-component objective over a mixed-variable design space. Existing metaheuristic approaches suffer from exponential search space growth with problem scale, while most deep reinforcement learning (DRL) methods adopt either purely continuous or discrete action spaces that fail to capture the mixed-variable structure of real-world 3D placement problems. We present LayoutRL, a constraint-aware DRL framework integrating mixed variables within a unified hybrid policy. As non-overlap constraints are inherently difficult to satisfy under sparse rewards in continuous action spaces, a minimum translation vector (MTV)-based action correction deterministically mitigates non-overlap violations at each step, while a Beta distribution ensures numerical stability for MTV-corrected actions near boundaries. To further improve solution quality, we introduce a fine-grained optimization stage that refines the DRL-generated solution. Experiments across N = 30, 50, 70, 100 object scales demonstrate that LayoutRL achieves zero constraint violations at all scales. Even at N = 100, where the metaheuristic baseline fails to produce feasible solutions, LayoutRL consistently achieves superior results, demonstrating its scalability and generalization across 100 test instances.

Introduction

Large-scale layout optimization is a fundamental challenge across PCB design, industrial plant layout, satellite configuration, and ship engine room arrangement. Mixed-integer programming offers optimality guarantees but does not scale beyond ~30 objects, while metaheuristics scale further at the cost of feasibility and confinement to 2D. Most existing DRL methods commit to a single action space—either purely continuous or purely discrete—and therefore cannot capture the mixed-variable structure inherent to real-world 3D placement, where coordinates demand continuous resolution and floor / rotation decisions are fundamentally discrete. LayoutRL addresses these gaps with a unified hybrid policy, deterministic non-overlap correction, and a fine-grained refinement stage.

Contributions

Hybrid Action Space

A unified policy that jointly handles continuous placement coordinates and discrete floor / rotation decisions, faithfully reflecting the mixed-variable structure of 3D placement.

MTV Action Correction

Deterministically enforces non-overlap during training. Action relabeling preserves PPO consistency, and a Beta-distributed policy guarantees numerical stability near action boundaries.

Fine-Grained Optimization

Uses the RL-generated solution as a warm start for local refinement, converging efficiently to high-quality, constraint-satisfying configurations.

Problem Formulation

We model 3D layout design as a constrained mixed-variable optimization problem. Given $N$ objects with predefined geometries, the design variables $\mathbf{D} = \{(x_i, y_i, f_i, r_i)\}_{i=1}^{N}$ collect placement coordinates, floor index, and rotation index for every object.

\[ \begin{aligned} \min_{\mathbf{D}}\quad & J(\mathbf{D}) = w_{\text{area}} A(\mathbf{D}) + w_{\text{dist}} C(\mathbf{D}) \\ \text{s.t.}\quad & g_k(\cdot) \le 0, \ \ k = 1,2,3,4, \\ & (x_i, y_i) \in [0, L_x] \times [0, L_y], \\ & f_i \in \{0, \ldots, F-1\},\ r_i \in \{0, 1\}. \end{aligned} \]

The objective combines layout area and connectivity distance, with $w_{\text{area}}=1.0$ and $w_{\text{dist}}=0.03$. Four geometric constraints define feasibility:

  • $g_1$ — XY-spacing: $d_{xy}(i,j) - \hat{d}_{xy}(i,j) \le 0$
  • $g_2$ — Floor-spacing: $d_{f}(i,j) - |f_i - f_j| \le 0$
  • $g_3$ — Boundary: object $i$ entirely contained in $[0,L_x]\times[0,L_y]$
  • $g_4$ — Alignment: aligned object's center within the reference bounding-box projection

Non-overlap is not added to $\{g_k\}$; it is enforced deterministically via MTV-based action correction (see Method).

Method

Hybrid Actor

A shared encoder branches into a Beta head for continuous placements (x, y) and categorical heads for floor index and rotation, allowing the policy to express precise spatial decisions and discrete structural choices simultaneously.

MTV Correction

Non-overlap is deterministically enforced at the environment level by minimally displacing objects via the minimum translation vector, providing dense and informative reward signals.

Action Relabeling + Beta Policy

PPO updates use the corrected action so importance ratios stay consistent, while a Beta distribution avoids the numerical instability of tanh-squashed Gaussians at boundaries.

Fine-Grained Refinement

The RL solution is used as a warm start for local optimization, refining placements to satisfy spacing, boundary, and alignment constraints while improving objective value.

Experiments

Across N = 30, 50, 70, 100 object scales, LayoutRL attains zero constraint violations while consistently outperforming metaheuristic baselines—including the large-scale N = 100 regime where baselines fail to produce feasible solutions.

Large-Scale Performance ($N=100$)

On the most challenging $N = 100$ setting, LayoutRL achieves the lowest objective value while producing zero constraint violations across all test instances. In contrast, the metaheuristic baselines routinely fail to return feasible solutions at this scale, highlighting the benefit of combining hybrid actions, MTV correction, and fine-grained refinement.

Scalability

As the number of objects grows from $N = 30$ to $N = 100$, LayoutRL maintains consistent solution quality and feasibility, whereas baseline performance degrades sharply. This indicates that the unified hybrid policy scales gracefully to the combinatorially harder regimes where classical search struggles.

Generalization

Evaluated on 100 unseen test instances with varying object-length distributions, LayoutRL delivers consistently high-quality, constraint-satisfying layouts. This demonstrates that the learned policy transfers beyond the specific geometries seen during training, a property that classical optimization methods lack without full reformulation.
